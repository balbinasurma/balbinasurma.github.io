Inline speed ​​skating. A non-olympic sport involving roller skating for speed skating (inline skates) of a given distance on a track or road as soon as possible. Roller skaters move in the middle speed of 30-40 km/h. Riding technique is significantly different from fitness riding on rollers. In order to achieve high speeds, skaters use the right one double push technique. Thanks to this maximum instantaneous speeds on rollers may exceed even 70 km/h. Competitors take part in competitions that are divided into long-distance (marathons and half-marathons) and short-distance (track and street).
